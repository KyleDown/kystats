Stat Explanations with calculators

KyStat Explanation

1. MT MeaningfulTouch:

Meaningful Touch is a category that sums up all the (FG+3P+FT+TRB+AST+STL+BLK) that are registered.

Times that team did something to the ball that affected the game in a positive way.

3P is counted for in FG but I think it's important to give it credit again since 3>2.

EX: (FG=47, 3P=0, FT=19, TRB=44, AST=33, STL= 8, BLK= 6) MT= 157

--------

2. PP PlayersPlayed:

Simply how many players played? This stat is more a side kick stat.

Notice if a team's lineup is becoming more tight.

Regular season will probably play more players than they do in the playoffs. Pre pre-season usually plays more players.

EX: Bulls - SP, MJ, HG, JP, BC, WP, BJA, SW, BH, CH, CL PP=11

--------

3. YE YearsEx:

This calculates all the years of experience the players have.

Shows how experienced teams are compared to their opponent.

EX: The Bulls in 1992 ECF1 have a YE=66 while there Opp the heat have YE= 27

--------

4. TP TeamsPlayed

What teams in the NBA/ABA are being represented

Teams are only counted once, so the max is 30. So this could be represented as a percentage as well.

The data to get this value could be saved and see if any team shows up more than others.

Ex: Bulls 1992 - 1.Bulls, 2.Spurs, 3.Jazz, 4.Kings, 5.Bucks, 6.Suns, 7.Clippers, 8.Pistons, 9.Hawks - TP=9

--------

5. TAS TotalAllStar

This will show how much star power a team has.

Total number of all star appearances. Some say this could influence referee descion making.

EX: Bulls 1992 - MJ=7 and SP=4 - TAS=11

--------

6. StartsStarting

How many starts does the starting lineup have?

This stat starts at the beginning.

This shows how much chemistry a team has together.

EX: Bulls 1992 - (MJ=82, SP=80, HG=79, JP=8, BC=3 +another 5 for that game) SS=330

--------

7. PAP PossibleAcumulatedPoints

This calculates all the attempts together to get a possible accumulated point total.

This category could be incorrect because if the team had multiple attempts in one run in reality they would have only scored once in the run.

This is a fun category because the higher it is, the worse it can be bad.

With this information it puts into perspective all the shots taken.

The equation for this category is ((FGA-3PA)x2)+(3PAx3)+FTA = PAP

EX: Bulls 1992 - (FGA= 86, 3PA=0, FTA= 24) PAP=196

--------

8. CE CoachExperience

This is how many years of experience a coach has at that time of the game.

This could be changed to show amount of wins a coach has instead of years coached

EX: Bulls coach was coaching for 2 years before the year 1992 so CE=2

--------

9. AssistsTOV

This category shows the ratio of being able to control the ball or not.

This stat is already a universal stat so still needs some adjusting.

EX: (AST = 33 /or- TTOV = 12) AT% = 2.73 and AT= +21

--------

10. BT BadTouch

A simple sum of the TOV and Fouls a team makes.

This needs more thought to be a fully effective stat.

EX: TOV=12, PF=21 making BT=33

--------

11. DS DefensivePoint

This stems from triple/double doubles and turns it into a team stat

Russel Westbrook would be scoring 3 points for his team every night

For example 5 players on the Celtics pre season game vs the 76ers scored in the double digits for points and 2 registered double digit rebounds.

EX: So the stat sheet would have a DS=7.

--------

12. DP DefensivePoint

This stat is interesting because it shows how defensive a team is.

On the stat sheet it looks like a good defensive night but it's important to see how everyone as a collective unit is on defense.

the max score is PlayersPlayed times 2

EX: Bulls 1992 - SP=1, MJ=2, HG=1, BJA=1, WP=1 so the DP=6
